## Gate Decision

**Gate 1 passed on in-domain synthetic mirror validation.**

The fair baseline comparison is now valid because frozen VGGT primary output and the layered secondary output are evaluated on the same validation split, same non-Lambertian pixels, same secondary-path GT, and same L2 point error:

- Frozen VGGT primary vs secondary GT on NL pixels: `5.887548`
- Layered secondary head vs secondary GT on NL pixels: `0.407244`
- Relative reduction: `93.083%`
- Layered mask accuracy on valid first-surface pixels: `98.437%`

This supports the narrow claim that the layered secondary head recovers synthetic mirror secondary-path geometry substantially better than frozen VGGT's primary pointmap on the same pixels.

## Remaining Limits

This result still does not prove:

- single-head fine-tuning is worse than layered prediction,
- Lambertian geometry is preserved after trainable fine-tuning,
- oracle-free performance is close to an oracle mask/plane variant,
- cross-domain or real-data generalization,
- superiority over Reflect3r,
- glass, glossy metal, or wet-floor handling.
